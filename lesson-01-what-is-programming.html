The Foundation

You Were Born Awake.

When you came into this world, you were a pure receiver. No filters. No labels. No story about who you were or what you were capable of. You felt the sun and knew it was warm. You cried when something was wrong and laughed when something was right. That knowing — that direct connection to Source — was fully intact.

Then the world got to work.

Before you could speak in full sentences, systems were already in place to shape how you would think, what you would believe, what you would accept as normal, and most importantly — who you would believe you were. That process is what we call programming.

"Programming isn't something they did to you once. It's something that runs every day — until you decide to look at the code."

— Nazir El, Ya Heard Me
Breaking It Down

What Programming Actually Is.

Programming is not a conspiracy theory. It is not abstract. It is a documented, deliberate process of installing beliefs, behaviors, and limitations into a person from birth — so that person operates inside a defined boundary without ever questioning the fence.

It comes through multiple channels, running simultaneously:

  • Education You were taught a version of history, science, and reality designed by institutions with specific interests. Not truth. A version of it.
  • Religion as Institution Spiritual authority was placed outside of you. You were taught to seek the Most High through intermediaries, rituals, and buildings — not within yourself, where the connection actually lives.
  • Media What is beautiful, successful, and worthy was defined for you through images, stories, and sound — most of which had nothing to do with your divine design.
  • Family The people who loved you most passed down their programming to you. Unconsciously. Because it was passed to them first. This is how cycles persist across generations.
  • Language The very words you use to think shape the limits of what you can conceive. Certain words carry histories that program your relationship to power, identity, and worth.
Key Insight

Programming works because it starts before you have the tools to question it. A child cannot evaluate the curriculum before accepting it. The installation happens first. The questioning — if it ever comes — has to fight through years of reinforcement to get a word in. That is not an accident. That is the design.

Research in developmental psychology consistently shows that by age seven, the core beliefs shaping adult behavior, self-worth, and worldview are already formed. Seven years old — before you could multiply, before you could read a contract, before you had any capacity to consent. The installation was complete before you had the tools to evaluate it.

What This Means for You

You Are Not Broken —
You Are Programmed.

This is one of the most important distinctions in this entire journey. Many people who come to this work carry shame — about their past choices, their patterns, their failures, the ways they have repeated cycles they swore they would break.

That shame is misplaced. You were not weak. You were not lacking. You were operating from installed software that was not written for your benefit. A computer running bad code does not need to feel ashamed of the output. It needs the code examined and rewritten.

  • Your capacity for divine connection was not damaged by the programming. It was buried under it.
  • Your ability to manifest and align with Source was not removed. It was redirected — toward systems and authorities that needed your energy more than you did.
  • Your identity — your true one — exists underneath everything that was installed. The Walk Back to Eden is the process of uncovering what was always there.

The first step is this: stop interpreting your limitations as evidence of who you are. They are not. They are evidence of what you were taught. And that is a critical difference — because what was taught can be unlearned. What is original cannot be destroyed.

The First Practice

Start Noticing.

Understanding programming intellectually is the first move. But this work is not academic — it requires practice. And the first practice is the simplest one: noticing.

For the next 24 hours, pay attention to every time you catch yourself thinking or saying any of these:

  • "That's just how it is."
  • "I'm not the kind of person who..."
  • "I could never..."
  • "People like me don't..."
  • "That's not realistic."

Every time one of those comes up — pause. Don't judge it. Just ask two questions: Where did I learn this? And who does it serve for me to believe it?

That pause — the space between a programmed response and your conscious awareness of it — is where your freedom lives. Everything starts there.

Prompt 03

Prompt 04 — After Your 24 Hours

Which phrase from the First Practice came up most? "I can't," "that's just how it is," something else? What does that pattern tell you about where your programming runs deepest?
